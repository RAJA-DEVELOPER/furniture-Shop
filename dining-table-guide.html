The Complete Guide to Choosing a Dining Table

The dining table is the heart of the home — a place where meals are shared, conversations flow, and memories are made. Choosing the right one can feel overwhelming given the endless options in shape, material, size, and mechanism. This guide walks you through every consideration so you can make an informed decision that balances style, function, and longevity.

Table Shapes: Which One Fits Your Space?

Rectangular

The most popular and versatile shape, rectangular tables fit comfortably in long, narrow dining rooms and can seat a large number of guests. They work well with both bench seating and individual chairs. The clean lines make them ideal for formal dining as well as everyday use. Allow at least 90 cm of width for comfortable place settings.

Round

Round tables are perfect for smaller spaces and for fostering a convivial atmosphere where everyone can see each other. Without sharp corners, they are also a safer choice for homes with young children. A round table works best in square rooms or as a dedicated breakfast nook centrepiece. For four people, look for a diameter of at least 110 cm.

Oval

Oval tables combine the best of rectangular and round: they offer a generous surface area and ample seating capacity while maintaining a softer, more organic silhouette that encourages conversation. They are a particularly good choice for narrower rooms where a round table would feel too tight.

Square

Square tables are ideal for compact dining areas and create a balanced, intimate setting for four. They also work beautifully as double-duty desks or craft tables in multi-purpose rooms. For comfortable seating, choose a side length of at least 120 cm.

Materials: Choosing the Right Surface

Solid Wood

Solid wood is the classic choice — warm, durable, and characterful. Oak, walnut, and teak are among the most popular species, each offering a distinct grain and colour profile. Solid wood tables develop a beautiful patina over time and can be refinished if damaged. They suit almost any interior style, from farmhouse to mid-century to contemporary.

Marble

Marble dining tables make a bold, luxurious statement. Natural stone offers unique veining and a cool, smooth surface that is stain-resistant when properly sealed. Marble works best in modern or minimalist interiors and pairs beautifully with metal or wood bases. Keep in mind that marble is heavy, porous, and can chip — it requires care and maintenance.

Glass

Glass tabletops create a sense of airiness and light, making them an excellent choice for small or dark dining rooms. Tempered glass is durable and easy to clean. The transparency also allows a sculptural base to shine. However, glass shows fingerprints and smudges readily and may not suit homes with very young children.

Metal

Metal tables — often steel, brass, or aluminium — bring an industrial or contemporary edge to a space. They are extremely durable, easy to maintain, and often more affordable than wood or stone. Metal works best in loft-style, modern, or eclectic interiors and is frequently paired with a contrasting material for the tabletop.

Size and Seating Capacity

As a rule of thumb, allow 60–75 cm of table width per person and a minimum of 90 cm between the table edge and the wall or furniture to allow for comfortable movement. For a table that seats six, look for a length of 180 cm; for eight, 220–240 cm. Always measure your room carefully and consider how many people you typically host before committing to a size.

Extension Mechanisms

If you regularly host larger gatherings but lack the space for a permanent big table, an extendable table is a smart investment. The most common mechanisms include butterfly leaves (which fold out from the centre), drop-leaf designs (which hinge up from the sides), and sliding extensions (stored within the table frame). Look for smooth, self-storing mechanisms that can be operated by one person — the best ones disappear entirely when not in use.

Pro Tips for Your Final Decision

Choose your table first, then your chairs — the proportions matter more than matching finishes. Leave at least 60 cm of knee clearance under the table. Consider how the table will be used daily, not just for holidays. And finally, invest in the best quality you can afford: a well-made dining table is a piece that will serve your family for decades.

Take your time, measure twice, and trust your instincts. The right table will announce itself the moment you sit down.
